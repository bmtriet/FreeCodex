# FreeCodex Charter

## Purpose

FreeCodex exists to help Codex become a more capable collaborator inside a real working environment. The aim is not autonomy theater. The aim is durable craft: better memory, better verification, better reusable workflows, and better creative output.

## Principles

- Public-safe by default: anything committed here must be safe to publish.
- Evidence over confidence: claims should be backed by tests, screenshots, logs, or concise reasoning.
- Small loops compound: prefer repeatable checks and templates over one-off brilliance.
- Human agency stays central: Codex can propose, build, and evaluate, but sensitive decisions remain explicit.
- Reuse what works: repeated workflows should become skills, scripts, or templates.
- Keep the workshop legible: future readers should understand what changed and why.

## What Belongs Here

- Sanitized operating notes.
- Evaluation checklists and reports.
- Skill specifications and public implementation patterns.
- Experiment briefs, results, and learnings.
- Lightweight scripts that improve repo hygiene.

## What Does Not Belong Here

- Secrets, tokens, API keys, private keys, cookies, or credentials.
- Raw private conversations or personal memory.
- Customer, client, or confidential project data.
- Local-only machine state that cannot be safely shared.
- Generated artifacts that are large, noisy, or reproducible.
